Differentiable Mappings

In calculus, everything has its geometric meaning, especially when a mapping is said to be differentiable at a point in its domain. Its rigorous definition is no more than a precise description of the intuition of smoothness.

Consider we have a mapping f1:Rm→R. we assume that there is a point p∈Rm, such that f1 is smooth at p. As f1 is smooth at p, intuitively, it means f1 tends to be the tangent plain of f1 at p if we magnify the details round f1(p). That is, there exists a neighbourhood N of p, such that f1 is smooth on N.

As R2 is a metric space, every open balls are open, so there exists a δ∈R>0, such that f1 is smooth on B(p,δ), where B(p,δ) denotes the open ball with center as p and δ as radius. Thus, if P1:Rm→R describes the tengent plain of f1 at p, then

limδ→0f1[B(p,δ)]=limδ→0P1[B(p,δ)].

In the term of elements, that is, for any t∈Rm with p+t∈B(p,δ),

limt→0Rmf1(p+t)=limt→0RmP1(p+t).

As P1 describes a plain, so it can be considered as a translated linear mapping. That is, there exists a linear mapping ϕ1:Rm→R, such that

P1(p+t)=ϕ1(p+t−p)+f1(p).

Thus, we have

limt→0Rmf1(p+t)=limt→0Rmϕ1(t)+f1(p).

Move the term f1(p) to the left hand side, we observe this limit,

limt→0Rmf1(p+t)−f1(t)−ϕ1(t)∥t∥Rm=0.

Then this equation describes the smoothness of f at p. In this case, in calculus, we say that f is differentiable at p.

Now, considere there are f1,…,fn:Rm→Rn such that for any i∈{1,…,n} such that fi is differentiable at p∈Rm; i.e., for any i, there exists a linear ϕi:Rm→Rn, such that

⟨limt→0Rmfi(p+t)−fi(t)−ϕi(t)∥t∥Rm⟩i=1n=⟨0i⟩i=1n.

By vector sum, we have

limt→0Rmf(p+t)−f(t)−ϕ(t)∥t∥Rm=0Rn,

where ϕ(t)=⟨ϕi(t)⟩i=1n.

Thus, we have our first definition.

Definition

Differentiable Mappings

Let f:Rm→Rn, and let p∈Rm. f is said to be differentiable at p iff there exists a linear mapping ϕ:Rm→Rn such that for any t∈Rm,

limt→0Rmf(p+t)−f(p)−ϕ(t)∥t∥Rm=0Rn.

Lemma

With the conditions above, ϕ is unique.

Proof. As f is differentiable at p, there exists an α:Rm→Rn with α(t)→0Rn, such that there exists an open subset U⊆Rm with p∈U, such that for any t∈Rn with p+t∈U,

f(p+t)−f(p)−ϕ(t)∥t∥Rm=α(t).

Assume there is another linear mapping λ, such that

limt→0Rmf(p+t)−f(p)−ϕ(t)∥t∥Rm=0Rn,

then, similarly, there exists a β:Rm→Rn with β(t)=0Rn, such that there exists an open subset U′⊆Rm with p∈U′, such that for any t∈Rn with p+t∈U′,

f(p+t)−f(p)−λ(t)∥t∥Rm=β(t).

Let γ=ϕ−λ. As ϕ and −λ are both linear, then

 γ(t)∥t∥Rm=α(t)−β(t)⟺limt→0Rmγ(t^)=limt→0Rm(α(t)−β(t))⟺ γ(t^)=0Rn.

As t is arbitrarily picked from U∩U′, and U∩U′ is open in Rm as U and U′ are open, the set

{t^:t∈U∩U′−p}

gives all possible directions in Rm. And as γ(st^)=0Rn, for any s∈R, γ(t)=0Rn constantly. Thus, ϕ=λ.
